Case Study

Fictional Lenguage Learning App, made as part of my
Google UX Design Certificate.

My Role:

UX/UI Designer, UX Researcher

Project Duration:

2 month

Responsibilities:

User research, Wireframing, Prototyping, Usability testing.

Overview

Lingo-Journey is an App and Responsive Website that allow users have the possibility of connecting with natives speakers of the language they are learning through chat and video call. People can also start learning a new language from the Alphabet. Additionally they can learn by choosing a specific topic and dive into it not matter what level they are. Besides, it has a search bar to look for words that they must not know.

What's the Problem

  • There is no App or Website that start teaching languages from the Alphabet.
  • Lack of conversational skills or not having the opportunity to speak often.

    • Solutions

  • Design an app that will improve basic education such as the Alphabet.
  • Design a chat and video call feature that allows users to connect and practice writing and speaking skills with natives speakers of the language they are learning.

    • The Challenge

    User Research

    I used Google forms to develop a questionnaire and then I published it on social media and in Survey Circle to reach my audience. I also send it to some friends that already were in the process of learning a new language.

    80% of people who answered my survey had already used an App to learn a foreign language, most of them have tried Duolingo and they also mention a few other Apps such as Babbel, Rosetta Stone or Busuu.

    The feedback received through research made it very clear that there is not App or website that start teaching a language from the Alphabet which is basic to identify new letters, sounds and pronunciation that doesn’t exist in their original language.

    The other problem I found was the lack of speaking practice, the big challenge comes when they face the real world situation and they feel embarrassed about their pronunciation when speaking.

    User Research Pain Points

    1

    "I've tried Duolingo, great app but the problem is that it starts right into words instead of starting with the alphabet. How can I say words when I don't know how to pronounce the letter correctly, or what if I don't know that a different language has letters with different pronunciations "

    2

    I had to stop learning Greek, Japanese, and Russian because there was no way I could learn their alphabets, so it would be great that an app helped me with those issues. Also, I wanted to learn Hebrew and Arabian, but I thought it would be impossible to learn their alphabets"

    3

    "I feel like my progress is slow and even after a long time I cannot have a basic conversation. This may come from not learning conversational skills or not having the opportunity to speak often. I also get embarrassed about my pronunciation while speaking"

    4

    "Grammar rules are sometimes frustrating, as well, especially when there are many exceptions to the rule"

    Competitive Audit

    Next I have compared 3 direct competitors (Duolingo, Babbel and Mondly) and one indirect competitor (Italki) to find out direction on gaps and opportunities to address on my personal product design.

    I have confirmed that none of my direct competitors offer to learn a language starting from the alphabet, neither give the opportunity to users to have a real conversation practice.

    While my indirect competitor does offer real conversational practice by choosing and paying a teacher, it's not specially good at given users the opportunity of having a real conversation with other partners.

    Those insights brings me a great opportunity to solve people problems on my design.

    Ideation

    I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the competitive audit. My focus was specifically on Alphabet Teaching, Chat feature and home page design.

    Low Fidelity Wireframes

    After drawing many papers wireframes and analyzing and comparing different rental homes websites, I started my digital low fidelity wireframes adapting solutions to users pain points. Then I have connected wireframes to get the low fidelity prototype.

    Usability Study Low Fidelity Prototype

    Once my prototype was done I was ready to conduct the usability study which help me to have a better understanding about participants needs. I have carried out this step with 5 participants, 3 of whom attended via zoom and 2 in person.

    Insights

    Participants want to be able to set a personal profile

    Participants had difficulty to find and use the flip the coin feature

    Participants suggested to have different options in the chat feature

    Refining the Design

    Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes like providing a clear section from the home screen to be able of setting a personal profile.

    The flip the coin feature wasn't very intuitive to use, so I decided to make it bigger and I placed it at the top of the chat as first thing to see when someone starts a conversation.

    Additional design changes included adding an option to “Show me who is connected” to the “Chat” page, and providing a diverse option of connecting with other users.
